# --description--

For a positive integer n, let σ2(n) be the sum of the squares of its divisors. For example,

σ2(10) = 1 + 4 + 25 + 100 = 130.

Find the sum of all n, 0 &lt; n &lt; 64,000,000 such that σ2(n) is a perfect square.
